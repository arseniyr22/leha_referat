Abstract

Static manifolds with boundary were recently introduced by Cruz and VitВҙorio in
the context of the prescribed scalar curvature problem in a manifold with boundary
with prescribed mean curvature [CV19]. These manifolds are also interesting from
the point of view of the general theory of relativity, where they arise as static
spacetimes satisfying Einstein equations.

In this thesis, we introduce electrostatic manifolds with boundary as a natural
generalization of the static manifolds with boundary in the presence of a nontrivial
electric field. We study their general properties and prove uniqueness theorems for
the non-compact case, relating electrostatic manifolds with boundary to ReissnerвҖ“
NordstrВЁom manifolds.

1.1 Thesis organization

The thesis is organized as follows.
In Section 2.1 we derive the defining equations of
electrostatic manifolds from the source-free EinsteinвҖ“Maxwell equations in static space-
times. In Section 2.2 we show how the defining equations for electrostatic manifolds with
boundary could be rewritten in the form of second order overdetermined elliptic equation,
then we show how that metrics on electrostatic manifolds with boundary arise as critical
points of an appropriate variational functional and then we establish a key lemma on the
general properties of electrostatic manifolds with boundary. In Section 3 we provide the
necessary background for Theorem 1.1 and Theorem 1.2.
In Section 4 we prove these
uniqueness theorems. In Appendix 5 we provide some auxiliary computations needed for
the purposes of the thesis.
