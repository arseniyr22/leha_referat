Abstract

The thesis is devoted to the study of orientation-preserving homeomorphisms
of three-dimensional manifolds with a nonwandering set consisting of a (cid:28)nite num-
ber of two-dimensional attractors and repellers, each of which is a disjoint union
of cylindrically embedded closed surfaces, the restriction of some power to each
of which is topologically conjugate to an orientation-preserving pseudo-Anosov
homeomorphism. Topological classi(cid:28)cation of model homeomorphisms realized on
each manifold admitting homeomorphisms of the class under study is obtained. It
is proved that a homeomorphism of the class under consideration is topologically
conjugate to a model mapping if and only if it has a one-dimensional invariant
foliation.

The results known for pseudo-Anosov homeomorphisms made it possible to
prove that the dynamics of mappings of the class G on a nonwandering set, up to
topological conjugacy, has the same structure as the dynamics of model mappings
of the set Φ. If an invariant one-dimensional foliation exists, then the dynamics
of mappings of the class G coincides with the dynamics of model mappings on
the entire manifold. In particular, it follows from Propositions 1.4 and 1.5 that
the restrictions of a mapping from class G to di(cid:27)erent connected components of
its nonwandering set are topologically conjugate homeomorphisms (for the same
power, equal to the period of the connected components of the nonwandering
set of the homeomorphism).
If we assume conjugacy with generalized pseudo-
Anosov homeomorphisms on the connected components of a nonwandering set of
homeomorphisms of the class under consideration, then we can obtain pairwise
topologically non-conjugate mappings on each connected component. In section 5
such example is constructed.

Conclusion

This work completely describes the dynamics of orientation-preserving homeo-
morphisms on three-dimensional manifolds with a nonwandering set consisting of
a (cid:28)nite number of cylindrically embedded two-dimensional surfaces, the restriction
of some power of mapping to each of which is an orientation-preserving pseudo-
Anosov homeomorphism. It is proved that the dynamics of homeomorphisms of
the class under study is similar to the dynamics of model mappings. The main
result of the thesis is the (cid:28)nding of necessary and su(cid:30)cient conditions for the
topological conjugacy of such homeomorphisms with model mappings. It is shown
that the obtained results do not generalize to homeomorphisms with generalized
pseudo-Anosov homeomorphisms in a nonwandering set.
