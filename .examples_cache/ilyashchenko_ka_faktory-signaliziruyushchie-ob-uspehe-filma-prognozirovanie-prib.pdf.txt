Abstract 

The  dynamic  and  lucrative  nature  of  the  creative  industries,  particularly  the  film 

sector,  requires  a  comprehensive  understanding  of  the  determinants  of  financial  success. 

This  study  employs  a  quantitative  approach  using  the  methods  of  data  mining,  semantic 

modeling,  thematic  distribution  analysis,  machine  learning  techniques,  and  econometrics 

analysis  to  develop  and  evaluate  a  predictive  model  for  estimating  a  film's  box  office 

performance  based  on  internal  production  signals.  By  analyzing  historical  data  from  "The 

Numbers"  database,  the  paper  aims  to  identify  key  factors  influencing  a  film's  financial 

viability  during  early  production  stages.  As  a  result,  the  main  signals  having  a  significant 

impact  on  a  film's  financial  success  at  the  box  office,  encompassing  emotional  content, 

thematic  coherence,  and  production-related  estimators  such  as  genre,  source  material,  star 

power,  and  release  timing,  were  determined,  culminating  in  the  validation  of  predictive 

models  like  random  forest  and  neural  networks.  The  study  contributes  to  empowering 

stakeholders in making informed decisions, advancing the field of film production analytics 

and  resulting  in  a  more  data-driven  and  financially  sustainable  film  production  ecosystem. 

Limitations  regarding  data  quality  and  model  transparency,  including  possible  future 

expansion of the model to incorporate external factors, additional metrics and data sources, 

are acknowledged, as well as future research agendas outlined to address these concerns and 

achieve a more comprehensive understanding of financial performance prediction in the film 

industry. 

Keywords:  Film  industry,  Financial  performance,  Predictive  model,  Machine 

learning, Internal production signals

Literature review 

While  considering  investing  in  a  movie,  forecasting  is  crucial,  as  it  enables 

stakeholders  to  anticipate  potential  financial  returns,  mitigate  risks,  and  make  informed 

decisions  regarding  resource  allocation,  production  budgets,  and  marketing  strategies. 

Hence, it is necessary to make a thorough review of relevant research papers related to the 

sphere as a basis for our paper. Based on these considerations, the Literature Review section 

is  devoted  to  the  analysis  and  comparison  of  previous  studies.  The  literature  review  is 

organized as follows: first, the research examining the theory of the signaling theory in the 

context  of  the  filmmaking  industry  was  considered.  Subsequently,  the  works  focusing  on 

various previously developed forecasting models, as well as factors most influencing movies 

financial  results  were  reviewed.  Finally,  the  papers  based  on  the  Internet  data,  Sentiment

Signaling Theory within the Filmmaking Industry 

Signaling theory, originating from information economics (Spence, 1973), serves as a 

crucial  framework  for  understanding  information  asymmetry  between  buyers  and  sellers. 

Within the movie industry context, signaling theory elucidates how movie producers utilize 

quality signals to mitigate this asymmetry and influence consumer purchasing decisions (Fan 

et  al.,  2021).  These  signals  play  a  pivotal  role  in  shaping  consumer  perceptions,  decision-

making  processes,  and  subsequent  box-office  performances.  Additionally,  the  signals  or 

factors  further  shaping  the  financial  success  of  the  movie  can  be  considered  by  potential 

investors  while  deciding  what  projects  are  worth  investing  into.  Signals  can  be  broadly 

categorized into internal and external. 

Internal signals encompass inherent product attributes such as star power, production 

budgets,  genre,  and  storyline.  These  elements  serve  as  indicators  of  quality  and  influence 

audience  expectations  and  preferences.  For  instance,  a  high-profile  cast  or  a  substantial 

budget may signal to consumers that the movie is of high quality and worth their attention. 

Conversely, external signals originate from outside  entities and include electronic word-of-

mouth, reviews, ratings, and marketing campaigns results. These signals provide additional 

information  to  consumers,  influencing  their  perceptions  and  decision-making  processes. 

Positive  reviews  or  word-of-mouth  endorsements  can  enhance  a  movie's  reputation  and 

encourage  audience  engagement,  while  effective  marketing  campaigns  can  generate 

anticipation and excitement.

Decision Support Systems in the Movie Industry. Forecasting Models Designed in the Prior 

Research 

Decision  Support  Systems  (DSS)  play  a  critical  role  as  the  basis  for  investment 

related  decisions  in  the  film  industry  due  to  their  inherent  capacity  to  provide  valuable 

insights in the initial stages of production. By leveraging DSS, stakeholders can make more 

informed  decisions  regarding  which  projects  to  invest  in,  mitigating  financial  risks  and 

maximizing  returns  (Power,  2002),  including  the  movie  industry.  Properly  designed  and 

using the relevant data, these systems offer a structured approach to evaluating factors such 

as  cast  popularity,  director  reputation,  genre  trends  and  other,  ultimately  enhancing  the 

efficiency  and  effectiveness  of  investment  strategies  in  the  dynamic  and  competitive 

landscape  of  filmmaking.  Eliashberg  et  al.  (2009)  present  a  practical  case  of  DSS 

implementation  through  the  SilverScreener  system,  demonstrating  its  effectiveness  in 

optimizing  revenue  through  movie  scheduling.  Despite  occasional  deviations  from  DSS 

recommendations,  managerial  decisions 

significantly 

contribute 

to  net  margin 

improvements, underscoring the system's robustness and flexibility. However, the study also 

emphasizes  the  importance  of  maintaining  management  confidence  in  the  DSS  and 

accommodating managerial needs in its development. 

A number of authors claim that neural networks are popular tools for predicting the 

box-office  success,  showcasing  the  superior  performance  of  these  models  compared  to 

traditional  statistical  methods,  achieving  significantly  better  classification  accuracy  and 

application for the decision making (Sharda & Delen, 2006; Delen et al., 2007; Zhang et al., 

2009). However, the reliance on neural networks raises questions about their interpretability 

and  the  potential  biases  inherent  in  data  collection  and  model  training.  Additionally,  the 

conversion of forecasting into a classification problem simplifies the complex dynamics of 

box-office  success,  potentially  overlooking  nuanced 

factors

Sentiment  Analysis  and  WOM  Social  Media  Data  Analysis  for  Enhancing  Forecasting 

Results 

In  examining  the  various  studies  on  forecasting  movie  financial  performance,  it 

becomes evident that sentiment analysis has become a focal point for researchers. A recent 

study conducted by Qiu and Zheng (2023) shed light on the prevailing issue of forecasters 

consistently underestimating or overestimating box office revenues, advocating for trimming 

strategies  to  address  bias  and  variance.  They  explore  two  trimming  approaches,  one 

generating  solely  positive  weights  and  another  removing  model  associated  with  negative 

9 

 
 
 
weights before optimization. Despite resulting in slightly lower forecast accuracy compared 

to unconstrained weights, their findings hint at the potential for refining trimming strategies 

to improve overall forecasting accuracy. While trimming may mitigate bias and variance, it 

can also result in lower accuracy compared to unconstrained weights, potentially limiting its 

practical applicability. 

An  intriguing  paper  by  a  group  of  Russian  researchers  uses  sentiment  analysis 

techniques  for  films  box  office  revenue  forecasting,  namely  by  examining  the  relationship 

between viewers' emotional experiences and box office receipts (Noakk et al., 2012). While 

factors analysis resulted in showing how emotions like sadness can notably impact revenue, 

their findings do not reveal a significant linear relationship between revenue and emotional 

evaluation, thus, the lack of a significant linear relationship challenges traditional forecasting 

methods.  This  prompts  a  reevaluation  of  how  emotional  factors  are  incorporated  into 

forecasting models and the need for nuanced approaches. 

The efficacy of integrating sentiment analysis with machine learning algorithms for 

box-office  forecasting  has  been  documented  in  the  literature  (Hur  et  al.,  2016).  Through 

experiments, the researchers demonstrate that audience-related factors, including sentiments 

expressed  in  reviews,  play  a  crucial  role  in  box-office  forecasting  both  before  and  after  a 

movie's  release.  Interestingly,  neutral  comments  were  found  to  be  more  influential  before 

release,  while  emotional  comments  became  more  important  post-release.  However,  the 

reliance  on  sentiment  analysis  raises  questions  about  the  robustness  of  such  methods, 

particularly concerning the subjective nature of sentiment interpretation and potential biases 

in data collection and analysis. 

Social  media  offers  a  rich  source  of  data  that  can  be  used  for  sentiment  analysis, 

allowing  researchers  and  businesses  to  more  accurately  gauge  public  opinion,  word-of-

mouth (WOM) and predict outcomes based on trends and reactions in real time. The article 

explores  the  utilization  of  the  data  collected  from  the  Twitter  publications  to  forecast  the 

performance of the movies shot in Bollywood, focusing on box office revenue and opening 

weekend  collections  (Gaikar  &  Marakarkandy,  2015),  highlighting  the  significance  of  pre-

release sentiment and hype, and demonstrates promising predictive accuracy in  forecasting 

box office revenue and opening weekend collections. Continuing the research in box office 

prediction using social media content, Chinese researchers present an alternative approach to 

movie  box  office  prediction  by  utilizing  WOM  data  collected  from  microblogging  and 

conducting sentiment analysis (Zhang, 2022). Their models demonstrate superior predictive 

power  compared  to  traditional  methods,  emphasizing  the  importance  of  social  media

social  media  data  may  overlook  other  influential  platforms  or  demographics,  raising

Moving  beyond  sentiment  analysis,  Hofmann  et  al.  (2017)  delve  into  the  roles  of

movie's  theatrical  run,  while  also  considering  the  moderating  influence  of  the  signaling 

environment  (Fan  et  al.,  2021).  Additionally,  the  results  of  the  study  document  that  star 

power significantly affects box office receipts in the week of the premiere, emphasizing the 

importance  of  high-profile  actors  and  directors  in  reaching  audiences.  However,  the 

emphasis on star power and eWOM volume overlooks other potential factors contributing to 

movie success, such as storytelling, production quality, and marketing strategies. 

Overall, while sentiment analysis and social  media data offer valuable insights  into 

audience  behavior  and  movie  performance,  researchers  must  critically  evaluate  the 

limitations and potential biases inherent in these methods. Therefore, for reliable and valid 

forecasting  in  the  film  industry  a  holistic  approach  integrating  multiple  data  sources  and 

considering broader contextual factors is essential. 

The  paper  aims  at  developing  a  tool  to  predict  the  factors  most  affecting  a  film's 

financial  success  for  filmmakers  and  investors  to  be  able  to  select  films  most  worthy  of 

investment.  

Formulation of hypotheses 

After analyzing the results of the prior research related to movie financing, we have 

formed a summary table representing the generalized findings of researchers with a focus on 

factors  that  play  a  key  role  in  predicting  the  financial  results  of  a  motion  picture.  A  table 

with summary results is presented in Appendix 1. 

For further process of data collection and modelling we have considered all possible 

factors of financial success of the film through the prism of signaling theory. Based on the 

analysis performed, the factors or signals were divided into the semantic subcategories. The 

11 

 
 
 
groups of signals  influencing the financial  success of the film were  identified according to 

the principle of systematization of various aspects of the creation and distribution of the film.  

  The "Content Factors" group includes elements reflecting the main characteristics of 

the  plot  and  content  of  the  film,  such  as  genre,  topic,  source,  whether  the  film  is  a 

part of a franchise or not, and emotions, which can serve as key signals to attract the 

attention of viewers.  

  "Production factors" cover the technical aspects of making a film, such as production 

method, film timing, acting, directorial power, and producer power that can influence 

the quality and success of a film at the stage of its creation.  

  The  “Commercial  Factors”  group  focuses  on  aspects  related  to  the  marketing  and 

distribution  of  the  film,  such  as  seasonality,  budget,  MPAA  rating  and  country  of 

release,  which  determine  the  potential  profit  and  success  of  the  film  in  the  film 

industry market. 

Consequently,  following  the  scientific  literature  review  and  on  the  basis  of  factors 

groups identified earlier, the following research hypotheses were developed: 

H1: Content Factors (Genre, Topic, Source, Franchise, Emotions) have a statistically 

significant effect on the financial performance of a movie at the box office. 

H2:  Production  Factors  (Production  Method,  Film  Time,  Acting  Power,  Directing 

Power,  and  Producer  Power)  have  a  statistically  significant  effect  on  the  financial 

performance of a movie at the box office. 

H3:  Commercial  Factors  (Seasonality  by  month,  Budget,  MPAA  rating,  Release 

Countries (number)) have a statistically significant effect on the financial performance of a 

movie at the box office.

Methods and Data 

The current study employs a quantitative approach with Machine Learning and Data 

Mining  technics  (François,  2008)  to  investigate  the  factors  influencing  a  film's  financial 

performance  during  its  early  production  stages.  This  methodology  leverages  the  power  of 

data  analysis  to  uncover  patterns  and  relationships  between  film  characteristics  and  their 

eventual box office success. 

Rationale for Quantitative Approach 

A quantitative approach offers several advantages in the context of our research. 

Firstly,  it  allows  us  to  analyze  a  large  and  comprehensive  dataset  from  "The 

Numbers", a well-established database encompassing information on over 39,000 films. This 

extensive  data  pool  provides  a  robust  foundation  for  identifying  potential  correlations 

between film attributes and financial outcomes.  

Secondly,  quantitative  techniques  such  as  machine  learning  and  data  mining  are 

adept  at  handling  complex  relationships  between  variables.  This  is  crucial,  as  a  film's 

financial success is likely influenced by a multitude of factors, some of which may be subtle 

or interconnected.  

By employing these techniques, we can uncover hidden patterns within the data that 

might not be readily apparent through traditional analysis methods. 

Research Design 

The following steps, which formed the basis of our entire research, were employed: 

1.  Data Collection and Preprocessing: 

  Source:  The  research  will  utilize  data  from  "The  Numbers",  a  comprehensive 

online  database  encompassing  information  on  over  50,000  films.  By  leveraging 

this  extensive  database,  we  aim  to  gain  a  multifaceted  understanding  of  the 

factors potentially impacting a film's financial trajectory. 

  Method: Web scraping techniques were employed to extract relevant data points 

from the website. A custom Python script was developed to navigate the website's 

structure and efficiently gather information while adhering to the website's terms 

of service. 

  Data Volume: 39,072 films were included in the collected raw dataset. 

2.  Preprocessing Stages:

  Initial  Cleaning:  Python,  a  versatile  programming  language,  was  employed

Data Collection: Web Scraping and Primary Data Acquisition 

We  decided  to  use  web  scraping  as  the  primary  method  of  data  collection.  Web 

scraping is the automated process of extracting information from websites using algorithms 

or specialized software programs known as web crawlers or bots (Lotfi et al., 2021). These 

programs allow researchers to navigate websites and gather specific data. The origins of web 

scraping can be traced back to the early days of the internet, in the 1990s (Gray, 1993). At 

first, web scraping was mainly used for commercial purposes such as price comparisons and 

market research. However, soon after, its potential for scientific research became apparent, 

and it has since become a vital tool for data collection within various fields. 
Web scraped data can be considered as primary  data if it meets the following criteria  (Ten 

Bosch et al., 2018): 

  Directness:  The  data  is  collected  from  the  original  source,  in  this  case  websites, 

without the need for any intermediaries. 

  Accuracy:  The data has been  collected in  a  reliable and accurate way, ensuring its 

suitability for research purposes. 

  Relevance:  The  data  relates  to  the  research  question  and  supports  the  study 

objectives. 

There are two primary types of web scraping: HTML-based and API-based. HTML-

based extraction extracts data by analyzing the HTML code on a website, while API-based 

uses  the  website's  Application  Programming  Interface  (API)  to  request  information  in  a 

machine-readable  form.  For  our  research,  we  primarily  use  HTML-based  web  scraping 

because  of  the  specific  structure  of  the  websites  we  target.  This  approach  allows  us  to 

accurately  and  efficiently  extract  the  data  we  need,  using  our  own  algorithm  to  collect 

information from the site, written in Python.

Data Calculus  

Powers estimation 

The process of calculating the power of the cast and crew involved several principles 

aimed  at  accurately  quantifying  their  contributions.  Initially,  the  data  pertaining  to  the 

composition of the cast and crew underwent transformation from string representations into 

structured  data  formats.  This  transformation  facilitated  subsequent  analysis  by  parsing  the 

data using the ast library, segmenting the information into discrete components, and creating 

new columns based on these components. 

Subsequently, the calculation of power was guided by several principles. The power 

of the leading cast members was determined based on the revenue group of the film and their 

individual  importance,  with  a  distinction  made  between  those  deemed  'IMPORTANT'  and 

others. Similarly, the power of lead ensemble members, supporting cast, cameo appearances, 

unnamed  roles,  uncategorized  roles,  narrators,  extras,  documentary  subjects,  and 

interviewees was calculated using analogous principles. In each case, the revenue group of 

the  film  was  considered  alongside  the  significance  of  the  individual's  contribution,  with  a 

higher weight assigned to those labeled as 'IMPORTANT'. 

The  process  involved  splitting  the  cast  and  crew  entries  into  separate  components, 

extracting the names of individuals, assigning power scores based on predefined criteria, and 

aggregating these scores to obtain a cumulative measure of their influence. This cumulative 

measure  was  then  integrated  into  the  main  dataset,  enabling  subsequent  analysis  and 

interpretation of the data.

Sentiment analysis 

To  enhance  the  understanding  of  emotional  nuances  within  textual  data,  sentiment 

analysis  techniques  are  employed,  including  the  utilization  of  lexicons  or  dictionaries  that 

assign  numerical  sentiment  scores  to  words  or  phrases  based  on  prior  linguistic 

investigations.  The  NRC  Word-Emotion  Association  Lexicon,  developed  by  Saif  M. 

Mohammad  from  the  National  Research  Council  Canada  (NRC),  serves  as  a  significant 

resource  in  sentiment  analysis  endeavors.  This  lexicon  encompasses  both  positive  and 

negative sentiment values alongside eight emotional categories, derived through the manual 

annotation of a dataset using the Maximum Difference Scaling technique (Saif, 2010). With 

14,182 unigrams classified as positive or negative and delineations of words' connections to 

emotions such as anger, anticipation, disgust, fear, joy, sadness, surprise, and trust, the NRC 

lexicon  facilitates  comprehensive  sentiment  analysis  across  diverse  textual  datasets.  Its 

availability  in  multiple  languages,  supported  by  automatic  translation,  further  broadens  its 

applicability.  Furthermore,  the  licensing  terms  of  the  NRC  lexicon  permit  its  unrestricted 

19 

 
 
 
 
usage for research purposes, providing researchers with valuable resources for investigating 

emotional dimensions within textual data.   

In  this  study,  sentiment  analysis  was  conducted  on  textual  data  using  the  syuzhet 

package  in  R  (Isasi,  2021).  Specifically,  sentiment  scores  and  emotional  associations  were 

computed for a set of texts. The textual data consisted of descriptions of various films, each 

encapsulating narrative elements  and thematic content. The syuzhet  package facilitated the 

sentiment analysis process by employing pre-compiled lexicons or dictionaries, such as the 

NRC  Word-Emotion  Association  Lexicon,  which  assigns  numerical  sentiment  scores  to 

words  or  phrases  based  on  previous  linguistic  research.  By  leveraging  the  get_sentiment() 

and  get_nrc_values()  functions  from  the  syuzhet  package,  sentiment  scores  and  emotional 

associations  were  extracted  from  the  textual  data.  These  scores  and  associations  provide 

insights  into  the  emotional  dimensions  and  sentiment  polarity  expressed  within  the  film 

descriptions, thus contributing to a deeper understanding of the underlying emotional content 

conveyed in the texts. 

Film classification 

The  classification  of  movies  was  conducted  through  a  series  of  steps  aimed  at 

identifying thematic clusters based on keyword associations extracted from movie synopses. 

Initially,  a  subset  of  data  containing  movie  titles,  release  years,  synopses,  MPAA  ratings, 

and keywords was selected by the presence of word "alien" to suppress the data volume for 

faster  calculations.  The  keyword  column  was  parsed  to  extract  individual  keywords,  and 

then  each  movie  was  represented  as  a  binary  vector  indicating  the  presence  or  absence  of 

each keyword. Subsequently, a network analysis and hierarchical clustering were performed 

to identify clusters of movies based on keyword associations.  

The  network  was  constructed  using  a  bipartite  graph,  where  movies  and  keywords 

were  represented  as nodes, and edges indicated the presence of a keyword in  a movie text 

batch.  Several  community  detection  algorithms  were  applied  to  the  network  to  identify 

cohesive clusters of movies. These algorithms included edge betweenness, fast-greedy, and 

walktrap  methods,  each  offering  unique  approaches  to  identifying  community  structures 

within  the  network  (Zhang  et  al.,  2018).  The  quality  of  the  clustering  was  assessed  using 

modularity, a measure of the extent to which the network is divided into clearly delineated 

communities. 

Furthermore, hierarchical clustering techniques were employed to explore alternative 

approaches  to  movie  classification  based  on  similarity  in  keyword  associations.  Different 

20 

 
 
 
linkage  methods,  such  as  complete,  average,  single,  and  centroid  linkage,  were  tested  to 

evaluate  their  effectiveness  in  identifying  cohesive  clusters  of  movies  (Shetty  &  Singh 

2021).  Each  linkage  method  resulted  in  a  dendrogram  representing  the  hierarchical 

relationships between movies based on keyword associations. We will examine the received 

topics and graphs in discussion section. 

The ultimate  goal  of this classification process  was to  uncover underlying thematic 

patterns and associations among movies, providing insights into their content and facilitating 

further  analysis  and  interpretation.  Through  these  methods,  movies  with  similar  thematic 

elements  or  genres  could  be  grouped  together,  offering  valuable  insights  for  various 

applications in film studies, recommendation systems, and audience segmentation.

of origin. These variables are carefully processed and transformed into appropriate formats 

to  facilitate data interpretation  and analysis. For  instance, categorical  variables like  release 

month and MPAA rating are converted into factor variables, while aggregate assessments of 

cast and production team attributes are computed to capture their overall contribution to the 

film.  

The received frame is then split into training and testing subsets, ensuring a balanced 

representation  of  data  across  relevant  thematic  variables.  Additionally,  logarithmic 

transformations are applied to key quantitative variables, such as revenue and film duration, 

to address skewed distributions and improve adherence to standard modeling assumptions, to 

suppress the distance for variables with different distribution scales.  

Machine learning models 

For final assessment of box-office we used several machine learning models for the 

revenue  of  cinematographic  productions  forecasting  (François,  2008).  We  begin  by

Root  Mean  Squared  Error  (RMSE),  Mean  Absolute  Error  (MAE),  and  R-squared  (R2)

compared to individual models.

the  neural  network  model  and  evaluate  its  performance  on  both  the  training  and  testing 

datasets, employing metrics such as MSE, RMSE, MAE, and R2.

effectiveness regarding our decision-making models.

model  that  accurately  predicts,  with  100%  precision,  the  films  in  which  investment  is 

warranted, and furthermore, accurately estimates the potential revenue. In other words, our 

hypothetical model predicts a film with $100,000.00 expenditure to generate $2,000,000.00

invest in a film, under the condition that they are guaranteed to receive all the film's earnings

if the film breaks even according to the model's estimates, they invest in it.  

Consequently,  we  will  not  only  obtain  machine  accuracy  of  the  model,  but  also 

examine  managerial  accuracy,  which  evaluates  the  financial  losses  incurred  by  investors 

when using our models.

Comparing and contrasting with the prior scientific literature 

The  emphasis  of  the  paper’s  methodology  on  internal  signals  such  as  star  power, 

production budgets, genre, and storyline is strongly supported by Fan et al. (2021), who also 

identify these elements as critical quality indicators that influence audience expectations and 

box-office  performance.  Both  studies  highlight  how  a  high-profile  cast  and  substantial 

budget can signal high quality to consumers, thereby attracting more viewers. While Fan et 

al.  (2021)  and  other  researchers  recognize  the  importance  of  internal  signals,  they  also 

integrate  external  factors  such  as  electronic  word-of-mouth  (eWOM)  and  reviews.  On  the 

one hand, the research by  Hofmann et  al.  (2017) and Fan et  al.  (2021) on the influence of 

star  power  and  eWOM  further  validates  our  findings.  These  studies  show  that  star  power 

significantly affects box-office receipts, particularly during the premiere week, which aligns 

40 

 
 
 
with  our  conclusion  that  high-profile  actors  and  substantial  production  budgets  are  crucial 

internal  signals.  On  the  other  hand,  with  our  focus  on  the  internal  characteristics,  certain 

essential relationships  could  be missed in  the results we obtained, that  were  considered by 

other researchers. 

The  integration  of  neural  networks  and  machine  learning  models,  as  discussed  by 

Sharda and Delen (2006) and Delen et al. (2007), further complements our focus on internal 

signals. These studies demonstrate that advanced algorithms, when fed with relevant internal 

data like production budget and star power, can achieve high predictive accuracy. Our paper 

supports  this  by  showing  how  internal  signals  effectively  forecast  financial  outcomes, 

particularly  in  the  early  stages  of  production  when  external  signals  are  less  measurable. 

However,  apart  from  the  researchers  above,  we  give  equal  weight  to  the  potential  factors, 

while the researchers specifically highlight star power and production budgets.  

Our  paper  also  intersects  with  the  sentiment  analysis  research  by  Hur  et  al.  (2016) 

and the social media analysis by Gaikar & Marakarkandy (2015). These studies highlight the 

impact  of  audience-related  factors  such  as  pre-release  sentiment  and  electronic  word-of-

mouth (eWOM) on box-office forecasting. While our primary focus was on internal signals, 

the external  factors  described in  the papers mentioned above  offer complementary insights 

post-release. This suggests that a holistic approach, incorporating both internal and external 

signals, can enhance predictive accuracy across a movie's lifecycle.  

Moreover,  the  study  by  Ghiassi  et  al.  (2015)  introducing  novel  variables  like 

production  budget  and  pre-release  advertising  expenditures  reflects  our  findings.  Both 

studies  confirm  that  these  early-stage  factors  are  pivotal  in  shaping  financial  forecasts. 

However,  apart  from  this  study,  that  leverages  machine  learning  and  hybrid  models  to 

predict  box-office  success,  our  research  adopts  a  more  traditional  analytical  framework 

focused on internal factors. 

Nevertheless, the specific context of our study may differ from others, that is also an 

important point to discuss. For example, Gaikar & Marakarkandy (2015) and Zhang (2022) 

emphasize the role of social media sentiment in different cultural contexts (Bollywood and 

Chinese  film  industry,  respectively).  Our  study,  by  focusing  on  internal  signals  applicable 

across various contexts, offers more universally applicable insights, though it may lack the 

cultural specificity that can be crucial in some markets.

Conclusion 

The  bachelor  thesis  is  aimed  to  determine  the  early-stage  production  factors  that 

significantly influence a film's financial success at the box office and to develop a predictive 

tool  to  aid  investment  decisions.  The  comprehensive  analysis,  which  included  semantic 

modeling,  thematic  distribution  analysis,  machine  learning  techniques,  and  econometrics 

analysis  has  yielded  valuable  insights  aligned  with  the  research  question  and  theoretical 

framework of the current paper. 

Firstly, the semantic analysis using the NRC lexicon provided a detailed exploration 

of  the  emotional  content  within  film  descriptions.  This  analysis  revealed  that  emotional 

factors  such  as  fear,  joy,  and  trust  play  a  crucial  role  in  shaping  audience  reactions  and, 

consequently,  box  office  performance.  For  instance,  films  like  "Avatar"  and  "Sinister" 

showed high ratings for fear and negative sentiments, which correlated with their dramatic 

and  intense  storylines.  Conversely,  "Kung  Fu  Panda  4"  exhibited  high  ratings  for  joy  and 

positive sentiment, reflecting its family-friendly and comedic nature. These findings support 

the  idea  that  emotional  estimators,  among  other  content-related  signals,  are  an  essential 

driver of audience interest and further financial success of the project. 

Secondly,  the  thematic  analysis,  employing  advanced  clustering  methods  such  as 

community  detection  algorithms  and  hierarchical  clustering,  uncovered  distinct  thematic 

patterns within movie synopses. These methods allowed us to group movies based on shared 

thematic  elements,  revealing  how  thematic  coherence  can  influence  box  office  outcomes. 

For  example,  the  hierarchical  clustering  identified  that  genres  like  adventure,  horror,  and 

musicals consistently formed coherent thematic clusters that performed well financially. This 

supports  the  notion  that  thematic  consistency  and  genre-specific  appeal,  among  other 

content-related signals, are critical factors in predicting a film's success. 

Moreover,  econometric  estimations  provided  two  robust  models:  one  based  on  real 

values  and  the  other  on  logarithmic  values.  In  Model  1  (real  values),  the  R-squared  value 

was 0.617, indicating that 61.7% of the variance in box office receipts is explanatory by the 

independent  variables.  Significant  predictors  included  production  method,  genre,  source 

material, running time, star power (lead and support), and seasonal release timing. Model 2 

(logarithmic values) showed an R-squared value of 0.605, with similar significant predictors. 

Both models highlighted the stable relationship between these factors and global box office 

revenues,  emphasizing  the  theoretical  premise  that  production  quality  and  strategic  release 

timing, among other production-related signals, are pivotal to a film's financial success. 

42 

 
 
 
 
Furthermore, considering the factors of accuracy and predictive power, random forest 

is shown to be the most effective model among other employed. The model's high R-squared 

values  and  low  error  terms  underscored  its  reliability.  Key  variables  such  as  production 

budget, release month, source material, genre, MPAA rating, and franchise status emerged as 

significant  predictors.  For  instance,  high-budget  productions,  releases  during  peak  months 

(May,  June,  July,  December),  and  films  based  on  well-known  sources  or  franchises  were 

consistently  associated  with  higher  box  office  revenues.  The  random  forest  model's 

transparency allowed us to assess the significance of these variables, further validating their 

impact on financial outcomes. 

Additionally, our neural  network model confirmed the robustness of these findings, 

providing  further  validation.  The  neural  network  achieved  high  accuracy,  with  metrics 

indicating strong predictive capabilities for both training and testing datasets. 

From  a  theoretical  perspective,  the  paper  contributes  to  the  understanding  of  how 

emotional engagement, thematic coherence, and strategic production decisions influence box 

office  success.  By  integrating  emotional  content  and  thematic  analysis  into  predictive 

models, we offer a novel approach to understanding audience preferences and film success. 

From  a  managerial  standpoint,  these  findings  offer  actionable  insights  for 

stakeholders  across  the  film  production  landscape.  Filmmakers  stand  to  benefit  from  the 

ability  to  assess  the  financial  potential  of  projects  during  the  early  stages  of  development, 

while  investors  can  make  more  informed  decisions  regarding  film  financing  based  on  the 

model's  predictions.  Ultimately,  this  research  holds  the  promise  of  contributing  to  a  more 

data-driven  and  financially  sound  film  production  ecosystem,  empowering  stakeholders  to 

navigate the complexities of the industry with confidence. 

Limitations and Future Research Agenda 

However, to enhance the research's robustness  and generalizability, we should state 

some limitations and considerations for improvement: 

Data Considerations 

While  "The  Numbers"  offers  a  valuable  source  of  box  office  data,  a  more  holistic 

understanding  of  a  film's  financial  success  can  be  achieved  by  incorporating  additional 

metrics.    This  includes  production  costs,  profit  margins,  and  revenue  streams  beyond  box 

office  collections,  such  as  streaming  services  and  merchandise  sales.    Data  from  these 

additional sources can be integrated alongside the box office information to provide a more 

comprehensive picture of a film's financial performance. 

43 

 
 
 
However, the quality of the data is paramount.  Web scraping inherently introduces 

errors, so implementing  robust data  cleaning and validation techniques is crucial  to  ensure 

the accuracy of our analysis. 

Furthermore,  the  research  currently  focuses  on  historical  data.    To  strengthen  the 

model's predictive power, we should explore methods to integrate external factors that might 

influence  future  box  office  success.  This  could  involve  incorporating  data  on  economic 

trends  or  evolving  audience  preferences.    By  considering  these  external  factors,  the  model 

can move beyond simply analyzing past performance and offer insights into a film's potential 

future financial viability. 

Algorithm Considerations 

Machine  learning  models  are  powerful  tools  for  pattern  recognition  and  can  be 

instrumental  in  identifying  the  factors  influencing  a  film's  financial  success.  However,  it's 

important  to  acknowledge  the  inherent  uncertainty  in  future  predictions.  Additionally, 

depending  on  the  chosen  algorithms,  the  model  might  become  a  "black  box,"  making  it 

difficult to understand the specific reasons behind its predictions.  To address this, utilizing 

interpretable machine learning models could  provide valuable insights.  These models offer 

greater  transparency  into  the  factors  driving  the  model's  predictions,  allowing  filmmakers 

and investors to make more informed decisions. 

Future Research Agenda 

By expanding the scope of analysis, a fuller understanding of a film's success can be 

achieved,  despite  an  initial  focus  on  early  production  data  being  a  valuable  insight.    This 

includes incorporating factors that influence success throughout the entire production cycle. 

By analyzing elements like directorial execution, marketing campaign effectiveness, 

and critical reception alongside early production data, we can gain a more holistic picture of 

what  drives  a  film's  financial  performance.    Directorial  execution  can  significantly  impact 

audience  engagement,  while  marketing  campaigns  play  a  crucial  role  in  generating 

awareness and interest.  Critical reception, both positive and negative, can influence a film's 

box office performance and ancillary revenue streams. 

Moreover,  quantitative  data,  while  valuable,  can  offer  a  limited  perspective  on 

audience  preferences.    Supplementing  this  data  with  qualitative  analysis  can  enrich  the 

model's  understanding  of  these  preferences.    Techniques  like  sentiment  analysis  of  social 

media reviews can reveal audience reception to specific themes, genres, and overall tone.

In conclusion, our research underscores the significant impact of content, production, 

and  commercial  factors  on  film  financial  performance,  providing  a  comprehensive 

understanding of  the  box office success  components and offering practical  applications for 

industry  stakeholders.  In  response  to  the  limitations  of  the  data  and  incorporating  the 

aforementioned considerations, the future  research can achieve a more robust and nuanced 

understanding of financial performance prediction in the film industry.
