Abstract 

Enhancing innovative competences has been considered as important learning goal in higher 

education due to growing needs for professional skills in the 21st century that makes the life 

challenging and stressful. To cope with this stress, individual needs to be resilient which can 

be  affected  by  personality  traits.  This  study  aims  to  determine  the  relationship  between 

personality  traits  and  innovative  competences  through  the  mediating  role  of  resilience.    A 

survey was created, and 521 responses were collected through self-reported questionaries from 

three developing countries namely, Russia, Pakistan, and Nigeria. The data was analyzed using 

Structural  equation  modeling  (SEM)  and  the  results  indicate  that  openness  to  experience, 

extraversion, agreeableness, and conscientiousness enhanced the resilience and that increases 

innovative  competences  including  creativity,  teamwork,  critical  thinking,  initiative,  and 

networking.  Whereas neuroticism decreases the  resilience, and  which negatively  affects  the 

innovative  competences.  Therefore,  we  can  conclude  from  the  findings  of  this  study  that 

individuals high on openness to experience and conscientiousness are better able to cope with 

the stress associated with failures because of their rich experience with similar situations and 

can  create  the  much-desired  solutions  to  problem.  Besides,  with  larger  social  network, 

extraverted people can increase their resilience level and ultimately enhances the innovative 

competence. In contrast, neuroticism personalities are overreactive and emotional instable that 

might  make  them  less  resilient,  therefore  reduce  their  level  of  teamwork,  networking,  and 

creativity.

Introduction 

The modern world has been craving for the innovation since the industrial revolution and its 

hunger has never been satisfied rather it has been growing ever since. Almost every aspect of 

the society strives for this phenomenon. The question is why is this so much important? The 

foremost relatable answer to this question is that it makes human progress (Pellicer-Sifres et 

al., 2017) whereby economies of the nation thrive. Innovation makes a significant contribution 

in the economic growth that overflows between countries, industries, and firms (Thakur-Wernz 

& Bosse, 2022). It is a buzzword, where every leader knows its importance, but they explain 

in their own ways. For example,  Skillicorn (2016) said, creativity is the ability to generate 

useful and novel ideas which works as a seed for innovation, but it is just an idea until it’s not 

applied or scaled. So, it is basically a capacity to conceptualize and implement innovative or 

creative ideas that have a real and meaningful impacts (Adepoju & Nwulu, 2021).  

Labor  force  needs  are  frequently  evolving  as  globalization  continues  to  influence 

national and international economies (Foster & Yaoyuneyong, 2016). The growing need for 

professionals  is  increasing  in  today’s  competitive  society  of  21st  century,  therefore,  the 

educational institutions are looking way forward to increase the innovation competence among 

their students. Because young generation is the future of the economy and they may work for 

organizations  after  their  studies  (Ovbiagbonhia  et  al.,  2019).  Indeed,  educational  system 

appreciate  creative  thinking  but  often  don’t  pay  attention  to  the  development  of  innovation 

competence which prepares students for the uncertain future  (Beghetto & Kaufman, 2014a). 

Instead,  they  should  help  students  to  develop  these  skills,  they  are  suppressing  it  but 

fortunately, this skill cannot be killed, because it is an inherent and natural aspect of human 

experience  (Beghetto  &  Kaufman,  2014a).  Therefore,  the  most  important  thing  is  the 

development of the environment that supports and encourage the enhancement of innovation 

competence among  students.  Where they have hypothetical  situations, updated and relevant 

curriculum and can learn human experiences based on uncertainty and able to think out of the 

box (Ovbiagbonhia et al., 2019).  

Uncertainty has association with risk and where there is risk, there is element of stress 

and  tough  times.  There  is  quote  related  to  the  innovation,  personality  traits  and  resilience. 

“Genius is 1 percent inspiration and 99 percent perspiration” (Thomas Edison, 1932). This 

quote can be explained as being initiative with creative idea that is risky is just a starting point, 

but 99 percent is the continuous struggle and efforts require to accomplish it. In the journey of 

continuous reconstruction, there can be challenges  and hardships throughout  the way. Then

understand the role of resilience in enhancing the Innovation competence and personality traits.

competence and personality traits.

Innovative Competences 

Innovation  is  the  crucial  and  important  factor  for  the  success  of  organization  and 

nowadays  companies are more focused on creativity and innovation  to  hire the individuals. 

Creativity  is  defined  as  creation  of  unique,  new,  and  creative  ideas  and  innovation  means 

successful implementation of those ideas (Farr & West, 1990). Thus, it means before being 

innovative, students should be creative enough to generate the ideas so later that idea could be 

successfully  converted  into  a  realistic  and  implementable  solution.  These  factors  help  to 

develop  the  competences.  McClelland,  (1987)  defined  the  competence  is  combination  of 

motivation, knowledge, skills, and abilities to increase the performance in any area or aspect 

of life. Currently, one of the major competences has been considered that is innovation. This 

competence is the cluster of competences including skills, abilities considered as innovation 

competences (Marin-Garcia, Andreu-Andrés, et al., 2016).  

According to (Farr & West, 1990) innovative competences is expressed as to create the 

original, relevant, implementable solutions of problems. Developing innovative competences 

among students has been important among higher education students because in recent years, 

the work environment is highly challenging due to technological change and complex demand 

system  (Chell  &  Athayde,  2009a).  Besides,  universities  are  being  aware  to  educate  the 

graduates  not  only  being  creative  but  also  innovative  that  can  be  able  to  generate  the 

implementable solution of the problems.  

Innovative competences have been considered as the important learning goal in higher 

education (Chan & Yuen, 2014) due to growing needs for professional skills in the 21st century, 

Besides,  innovative  competences  are  considered  that  can  integrate  the  skills,  attitude  and 

knowledge and innovative students  are found to have good  leadership skills, are motivated 

towards tasks, creative, self-persistent, take calculated risks and like to work in complex and 

ambiguous environments that can appear more challenging to them. Therefore, universities are 

looking for ways that can foster innovative competences among students. Based on Dalke et 

al., (2007) one of the ways can be a learning environment that is more focused on hands-on

Pérez-Peñalver et al., 2018).  

Creativity 

Creativity is defined as the generation of novel ideas, its improvement, and problem-solving 

skills  (Pérez-Peñalver  et  al.,  2018).  It  can  be  a  crucial  factor  that  can  enhance  innovative 

competences.  Because  when  a  problem  is  identified  then  there  is  a  need  for  an  innovative 

solution (Antonietti et al., 2011). Innovative solutions can be developed when students are open 

minded for new ideas and can see it from different perspectives and become aware of many 

factors  that  could  affect  the  decision  including  possibility  and  constraints.  Besides,  it  can 

connect  the  several  events  and  factors  that  can  be  useful  to  reach  a  decision  making  by 

combining  the  old  and  new  experiences.  Creativity  could  be  like  questioning  assumptions, 

leveraging the power of imagination, and challenging the beliefs and stereotypes.   

Critical thinking 

It is defined as the process after the creation of ideas. It includes evaluating the ideas based on 

their advantages, disadvantages considering risk in it (Saatci & Ovaci, 2020a).  Those students 

who think critically would like to solve real life ambiguous and complex problems that can be 

highly challenging for them tend to be more innovative and willing to take more risks (Tabak 

& Barr, 1999).

Resilience 

To enter in the adulthood from the age of 19-29 years is highly challenging and stressful that 

includes a lot of ups and down but also open the doors of various possibilities (Arnett, 2015). 

Further, this phase of life seems to be highly complicated because it includes self-exploration 

and self-discoveries, and it became more complicated when students enter in university life. 

The problem they face is like somehow similar at the beginning of  years: The environment 

adaptability, to learn new methods and learn different things, deal with hectic schedule, can 

easily be frustrated because of less time available. This inflexibility of time leads to lower the 

performance of students which ultimately decreases the motivation and concentration among 

students. The individual who finds the ways to cope these challenges are being more resilient 

and  become  able  to  manage  and  overcome  the  stress  and  difficulties  in  their  adult  stage.  

Resilience  is  defined  as  the  set  of  personal  qualities  which  enable  person  to  flourish  in  the 

adversity (Connor et al., 2003).   

The explanation and definitions of resilience has been relatively challenging over the 

period  for  academicians  because  it  includes  various  contexts,  namely  educational  context, 

community, organizational, clinical, and military. Firstly, this concept was used in physics to 

define  the  properties  of  elastic  objects  then  further  it  extended  for  other  fields  (Dyer  & 

McGuinness, 1996). Generally, the definition of resilience is divided into two group thoughts. 

The first group defines that resilience is the personality trait and considered as development 

factor (Lee et al., 2012). The second group defines that resilience is dynamic process and can 

be  influenced  by  the  external  factors  or  environment  (Lee  et  al.  2013).  It  is  process  which 

defines that the ability to adopt well the adversity and stress. Those students have great level 

of  resilience  then  they  can  cope  with  adversity,  stress,  and  difficult  situation.  While  some 

students are more resilient than others which can leads to lower performance, motivation and 

even in worst case university with drawl or dropout.  Therefore, researchers have found that 

those individuals who are resilient can engage and adopt and cope with adversity and turn the 

stressful  situation  into  opportunities  which  can  be  useful  for  learning  and  development 

(Campbell-Sills and colleagues, 2006) and can lead to better academically also increases the 

motivation among students.

Personality traits 

One of the most essential themes in psychological research is Personality (Danny Azucar et al. 

2017). Research has shown that personality has been demonstrated by the foretelling of many 

exposures of life, including job performance (Andrew Nealet al. 2011), health (Stephen Soldz 

& George.E, Vaillant,1999), political attitudes (Alan S.Gerber et al. 2010), academic success 

(Meera  Kommarraju  et  al.  2009),  subjective  wellbeing  (Natalie  Hayes  &  Stephen  Joseph, 

2002), Online behaviors (Shaojung Sharon Wang, 2013) and social status (Ameron Anderson 

et al. 2001). Human Personality is not only a composite of one factor, and this is very subjective 

in  nature therefore it is  considered as a  complex element  of human. According to  Sigmund 

Freud, the first person who gave the theory on personality in 1923. He divided the personality 

into three parts, the id, ego, and superego and according to Freud’s psychoanalytic theory, all 

these components develop at different stages of human lives like id is the innate element of the 

personality,  whereas  Ego  develops  from  the  Id  and  is  the  decision-making  element  and 

Superego is the voice of conscience (moral sense).  

The term personality is defined in different ways and many Psychologists have worked 

on this term for decades and added value addition in this definition. Traits are the attributes 

that typically belong to a person and have different features. Personality traits are the individual 

factor that varies from person to person based on the characteristic sets of the behavior, thinking 

and  feelings.  It  holds  the  chain  of  factors,  opinions,  moods  and  attitudes  that  is  easily 

understood while interacting and communicating with other people (Philip S.Holzman, 2022).  

More specifically, from human to students, they are the most important part of every nation, 

and this part is also an important stage of human life. From primary to post graduation or all 

stages  of  education,  they  behave  differently,  their  worldviews  are  different  in  various 

educational  and  social  settings  because  they  have  distinguishable  personality  traits  (Soraya 

hakimi et al. 2020). Understanding the personality traits among students is considered as an 

important factor, because it clarifies the many insights of students' behaviors in the real world. 

Personality traits and cognitive abilities are directly or indirectly correlated with their academic 

achievements (Paunonen & Ashton, 2001). And student’s academic achievements lead to many 

different  elements  like  their  motivation,  positive  attitude  towards  corporate  life  and  can  be 

valuable resources of any Economy. There are cumulative effects of academic performance 

and  school  adjustments  with  the  course  of  time  that  have  continuity  effects  on  personality 

(Caspi et al. 2005).

In addition to these definitions, after years of effort, Psychologists have created the five 

core dimensions of personality traits and became the main kinds of the personality structure 

(Xiaojie  Zhang  et.  al,  2020).    Personality  traits  are  individualistic  different  characteristics 

among  students,  how  they  think,  behave,  and  feel  differently.  There  are  many  ways  of 

measuring  personality  traits  but  among  the  best  ways  to  measure  those  traits  are  Big  5 

personality traits (Preethi CM and R G Priyadarshini, 2018). Personality of a person can be an 

external or internal factor that is affecting the emotions and it is not only the isolated part of 

human learning it  can be evaluated  based on the traits  of the society, groups and at  certain 

levels  of  humankinds  (Oguzhan  and  Sebnem,  2015).    In  Psychological  trait  theory,  Big  5 

personality  traits theory was developed in  1949  by D.W. Fiske and it was expanded by the 

other  researchers  later  onwards  and  these  Big  5  personality  traits  include  Openness, 

Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness and Neuroticism. 

Openness to experience 

Openness to experience is simply how open minded a person is to new ideas or experiences, 

and it is a tendency to  be creative and aesthetically sensitive (Xiaojie Zhang, 2020). It  also 

reflects  broad-mindedness  of  individual,  permeable  awareness,  in  depth  attitudes  (Hakimi, 

2011). Openness is further divided into its sub traits like Imagination, Emotionally, Intellect, 

liberalism, Adventurousness and Artistic interests (Srivastava, 2018). The person who scores 

high on openness will be categorized as intellectual, aware of his feelings, strong in imagination 

and thinks in non-confirming ways. On the other hand, if a person scores low on openness they 

will be categorized as down to earth and do things in a straightforward way (Srivastava, 2018). 

Conscientiousness 

Conscientiousness is defined as the person extent to which it is responsible and achievement 

oriented (Jirásek & Sudzina, 2020). This trait is considered as one of the most important traits 

in personality (Ashton, 2022). When conscientiousness level is high that could lead to positive 

outcomes in many aspects of lives including student and work life (Ozer & Benet-Martinez, 

2006).  Due  to  technological  change  and  emerging  new  fields,  people  want  to  change  their 

personality  voluntary  that  increases  the  people  desires  to  enhance  the  conscientiousness 

(Hudson & Fraley, 2015).  

14 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Extraversion 

Extraversion is one of the dimensions of personality trait and it is explained as the extent to 

which person is self-oriented or socially oriented (Feist, 2019a). When individuals have the 

trait of extraverts that tend to be talkative, cheerful, confident, enjoy spending time with others, 

good at leadership skills and considered more satisfied from lives (Yao & Li, 2021a). Whereas, 

when person has low level of extraversion considered as introverts and they are more Quiter, 

un-social, keep themselves behind the background and enjoy being alone.  

Agreeableness 

Agreeableness is defined as extent to which person is friendly, considerate and interact with 

others  in  friendly  manner  (Saatci  &  Ovaci,  2020b).  Besides,  it  is  described  as  having 

harmonious relationship with others, likeable, kind, warm and pleasant. These individuals are 

motivated  to  maintain  the  positive  relationship  with  others,  and  they  could  have  good 

networking and teamwork skills (Graziano & Tobin, 2009). Being agreeableness could have 

negative impact on the personality because to avoid the conflict they might not be able to think 

creatively and doubt on themselves and their ideas. The people those are low on agreeableness 

tend to be consider as unfriendly, self-centered, and uncooperative (Chang et al., 2014) and 

they share their ideas to other team mates regardless of caring about conflict which can affects 

the relationship with the teammates.   

Neuroticism 

Neuroticism is about the extent the person is emotionally stable or emotionally unstable.  These 

neurotic people experience negative feelings and overreact to the situation  (Widiger, 2009). 

When the individuals  have high  score in  neuroticism that means they will experience more 

negative  emotions  than  the  average  person  like  fear,  anxiety,  guilt,  anger  and  depression 

(Widiger, 2009). Most of the time, they poorly respond to stress of environment and consider 

the ordinary situation as threatening that could leads to frustrations. These individuals often 

tend to be shy, self-conscious, and quite difficult for them to control the urges and impulses 

which can make them sad or upset (Widiger, 2009).

Theoretical framework  

Theory of resilience 

Resilience  theory  described  as  the  stress  coping  ability  that  could  managed  the  stress, 

depression, and anxiety in more effective way. It is the motivational force that drive the human 

being to pursue wisdom, altruism, and harmony with the force of spiritual strength. According 

to Benson, (1997) resilience helps to identify the individual traits and strengths that could help 

the human being to survive in the face the adversity. Besides, Emmy Werner (1982) described 

the  personal  characteristics  that  includes  socially  responsible,  extrovert,  conscientiousness, 

tolerant, adaptable and have good self-esteem. Whereas Norman Garmezy (1991) explained 

the  resilience  theory  from  different  perspective  and  the  characteristics  he  suggested  self-

discipline, internal locus of control, critical thinking skills, self-esteem, and problem-solving 

skills. There are many resilient qualities describes by authors included creativity (Simonton, 

2000). These list of traits, states and characteristics has been exhaustive therefore the paradigm 

shift  from  identification of risk factors to  focus  on personal  strength  and qualities has been 

significant.  

Resiliency theory explains that there is force inside everyone which drive them to seek 

wisdom,  self-actualization,  harmony,  and  altruism  with  the  help  of  spiritual  strength.  In 

educational system, resilience helps the students to grow and know themselves by body, mind 

and spirit through innate qualities or strength. This helped the students to handle the adverse 

situation  without  being  overwhelmed.  Besides,  it  produces  the  child-like  nature  among 

individuals  and  create  the  self-love  that  drives  them  to  enhance  the  creativity,  being 

adventurous and curious in  life. Resilience can be strengthened by the personal  qualities  or 

personality traits which can help the students to become more innovative and creative. Whereas 

(Rutter, 2013) explained the resilience theory consider the individual difference in personality 

traits;  genetics  creates  the  difference  in  the  response  of  each  person  towards  the  risk  and 

protective factors because same person can respond differently in different  situation, so it is 

more affected by environment change. Similarly, (Luthar, 2013) align with his point of view 

and  he  explained  that  resilience  is  dynamic  process  that  is  related  to  positive  adaption  of 

environment  in  case  of  adversity.  Therefore,  we  can  say  that  resilience  is  affected  by 

personality  traits  and  by  the  environment  which  can  affects  the  students’  innovative 

competences in-terms of creativity and critical thinking skills.

Investment theory of creativity 

This study has been used the basis of investment theory of creativity proposed by Sternberg 

(2006) with the collaboration of Todd Lubert. He proposed that creativity involves largest part 

of decision. People are considered creative when they buy low and sell high in terms of ideas. 

Buy low means creative people propose the ideas which are novel and can get high level of 

resistance.  But  they  convince  people,  and  their  ideas  start  getting  acceptance,  so  those 

individual sell at high  and reap out  the profits then again  move to  another new idea. These 

creative people resist  to follow the crowd instead they create their own direction and try to 

achieve it. Creativity is learned behavior not inborn behavior, they set their own attitudes and 

work in that direction by taking the risks, face the obstacles and examine their own thoughts 

which can make the hurdle in the creative process. Based on investment theory of creativity, 

these set of attributes are teachable and students can adopt these attributes and enhance their 

creativity.   

Investment  theory  of  creativity  is  explained  based  on  six  components  those  are: 

Abilities,  Style  of  thinking,  environment,  motivation,  knowledge,  and  personality  traits. 

Abilities includes to see the problems from new perspective and try to solve it with new ideas 

and then evaluate those all ideas and try to pursue the feasible one. Afterwards, try to convince 

others the value of that idea and take their support and eventually sell that idea to those people. 

Second component is style of thinking, that creative person is more open minded and open to 

new ideas and think in a way that can valuable and fit for its own purpose. Third component is 

environment that creative people need supportive environment to flourish their ideas, therefore, 

in  universities  teachers  should  provide  supportive  environment  to  students  to  enhance  the 

creativity.  Besides,  creative  people  love  to  do  what  they  do  that  is  their  main  cause  of 

motivation and that enhance the creativity. Creativity cannot be totally based on novel ideas 

rather it is also affected by existing knowledge and new information. Finally, personality traits 

play a very important role in the creativity, these individuals are risk seekers, and they love to 

take risk and they do not try to do the things through conventional way rather they buy low and 

sell at high and show the readiness to take the sensible risks and challenges and overcome the 

obstacles  and  have  a  sense  of  clarity  for  their  ideas.  Overall,  these  six  components  can  be 

adopted which can make the individual creative and innovative.

Personality traits and innovative competences with the mediating role of resilience 

Resilience is considered as process that can demonstrate itself by the response of external life 

events and personality profiles of individual (Dyrbye & Shanafelt, 2012). Besides, resilience 

is defined as the personality characteristics that can reduce the negative effects of stress and 

help in adoption. The relationship between resilience and personality traits has been discussed 

by  various  authors  that  provides  higher  level  of  conformity.  It  has  been  investigated  on 

university students and found significant relationship between resilience and personality traits 

including  extraversion,  neuroticism,  conscientiousness,  agreeableness,  and  openness  to 

experience (Campbell-Sills et al., 2006). Moreover, resilience is not only explained by fixed 

personality  or  personal  characteristics  rather  it  can  be  dynamic  developmental  process  that 

reflects the positive adoption even in the case of severe adversity (Luthar, 2003). She explained 

that it involves the process and conditions that interacts in a way to deal with adversity that 

determines the resilience which can affects the innovation competences of students.  

Openness to experience and innovative competences with the mediating role of resilience

encourages the creativity. Moreover, Beaty et al., (2016) & Feist, (2019) found the positive

Methodology 

In this section we discussed about the research philosophy, research approach, methodological 

choice,  strategy,  time  horizon,  techniques  of  data  collection,  construct  of  questionnaire, 

measurement scale for big five personality traits and Innovation competences. Apart from these 

all, reliability and validity of the data was explained. Finally, explanations and justifications of 

the data analysis techniques were provided. 

Research Design 

 Research philosophy is a way to inquire the reality or struggle to see the truth through various 

patterns  (Winit-Watjana,  2016).  To  answer  the  research  questions  through  selecting  an 

appropriate methodology, this research is following the positivism philosophy. Primary goal 

of positivism paradigm is to generate a casual or explanatory connection that will ultimately 

anticipate the aspect of the questions (Park et al., 2020). Further, Hypothetico-deductive model 

of science was aligned with positivism philosophy, and it will work as a lens that filter out the 

research one by one. First its start will the theory that is the part of the literature review, second 

building the hypothesis for testing, third is designing an operation through operationalizing the 

variable and fourth is conducting an empirical study (Park et al., 2020). So basically, this sums 

up the deductive research type. 

This study adopted a deductive research approach and a quantitative methodological 

choice.  In  this  choice,  we  measured  the  impact  of  big  five  personality  traits  on  innovative 

competences and tested the hypothesis. Data type is primary data and strategy is the survey. 

Data Collection & Sampling 

Data was collected through electronic survey that was created on google forms and distributed 

to the students through various platforms. Three countries were considered for this study these 

are Nigeria, Pakistan, and Russia. All participants were the students and from different fields 

having  qualifications  of under-gradation,  graduation,  post-graduation,  and  certified  degrees. 

Majority of the data were collected through social media networks to get responses from the 

participants  from  3  countries  like,  WhatsApp  broadcast  groups,  VK  platform,  Facebook 

messenger  and  Telegram.  There  were  also  the  other  ways  to  approach  the  students  like 

professors asked from their students to fill the questionnaire during classes and we approached 

the  students  during  break  time  of  university  by  describing  the  research  purpose  to  fill  the 

questionnaire.

Big Five Personality Traits 

Big five personality trait is an independent variable and there are also five constructs that are 

used to define this independent variable based on empirical research. There are a lot of research 

have been conducted on the big five personality traits in the history and there are many versions 

of scales are available in those studies. Some have used 100-item questionnaire where some 

have used 60-item questionnaire with 5/7- or 9-point scale (Goldberg, 1992; Hayes & Joseph, 

2003). In this research, short version of questionnaire was used with 10-item and 5-point scale 

ordered from absolutely disagree to absolutely agree (Gerber et al., 2010; Gosling et al., 2003). 

Each personality trait is consisted of two items including one reversed item. To increase the 

response  rate  and  reduce  the  size  of  the  questionnaire,  single  item  measure  is  used  for  the 

resilience  that  how  well  this  single  item  captures  key  characteristics  of  the  construct  being 

examined and how useful it is to practitioners (Fisher et al., 2016).  

Resilience 

Resilience  is  used  as  a  mediator  variable  between  innovation  competences  and  big  five 

personality  traits  which  is  our  dependent  and  independent  variable.  There  is  no  previous 

research on resilience where it is used as a mediator between these mentioned variables at same 

times.  Resilience  is  measured  with  the  Brief  Resilience  Scale  (BRISC).  There  are  several 

versions  of  BRISC  (Catalano  et  al.,  2011;  Dodd  et  al.,  2015;  Shin  et  al.,  2012)  and  in  this 

research 3-item scale is used which is almost a short version of CD-RISC (Charoensap-Kelly 

et  al.,  2021).  The  5-point  Likert  scale  is  used  to  measure  the  resilience  ranging  from  (1)  is 

absolutely  disagree  and  (5)  is  absolutely  agree.  For  the  reliability,  Cronbach’s  alpha  is 

measured, and it is 0.74 which means scale is reliable to use.

Data  was  consisted  of  521  total  observations.  Further  for  the  final  analysis  we  took  three 

variables for this research. This variable selection was based on the previous work done by

Pre-processing the data is one of the important steps that makes analysis more useful, 

and  this  step  has  further  sub-steps  and  techniques  that  are  commonly  used  by  researchers 

globally. First, in our data there were no missing values, but data was consisted of few outliers

Data Analysis Techniques 

To answer the questions through analyzing the data, first we measured the reliability of scales 

of innovative competences and resilience with the help of using Stata Software. For reliability,

Reliability 
Those people are considered as reliable whose behavior can be predictable or can be relied on 

consistency. Likewise, this concept is also followed in the research field, the extent to which 

the instrument used in the research, yields the same results on repeated measure will show the 

reliability of the research (Putka & Sackett, 2010). It is not possible to give the exact figures to 

measure the reliability but there are three estimations that are commonly used for to calculate 

the reliability. The three attributes of estimating the reliability are Homogeneity, Stability and 

Equivalence and there are several ways to measure each attribute (Heale & Twycross, 2015) 

The nature of this study asks to consider the Homogeneity attribute which means the 

internal consistency among the various items that together represent the one construct in the 

research. In our research, both independent and dependent variables are composed of many 

items  and  each  item  is  composed  of  various  questions  that  are  explained  earlier.  So, 

28 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
homogeneity means the extent to which various items reasonably team together to measure a 

single  characteristic.  Like  agreeableness  is  one  of  the  constructs  of  the  big  five  personality 

traits and to determine this we have two items “is generally trusting” and “tends to find fault 

with others”. 

Cronbach’s α is one of the most used techniques used in to measure the homogeneity 

and ultimately it represents the how reliable the instrument is. In this study Cronbach’s α is 

used to test the internal consistency among innovative competences constructs and resilience 

as well. It  tells  the correlation  in  every combination among  the set  of items.  It  is  measured 

between 0 and 1. The acceptable score for the Cronbach's α is 0.7 or higher than this, which 

means that instrument is reliable to research further (Heale & Twycross, 2015). Reliability for 

Innovation competence and Resilience has been tested and that is more than 0.7. This means 

that scales are valid.   

Validity 

 Validity describes how well the data gathered corresponds to the investigation’s actual area 

(Ghauri & Gronhuag, 2005). It is basically used to check the accuracy of the data. There are 

four main types of the validity namely, content validity, construct validity, criterion validity 

and face validity and each type has further sub-type (Heale & Twycross, 2015). In this study, 

validity is required to check because it describes that how well the concept, idea or behavior 

transformed or translated into operating reality and functioning.  

Further, Confirmatory factor Analysis method is a technique that is used in this study 

to check the validity.  It is a type of structural equation model that deals with the relationships 

between factor and indicator (Moore, 2012).  

Structural Equation Modelling (SEM) 

To identify the effect on innovation competence through personality traits and resilience, the 

structural  equation  model  is  used.  The  normality  of  all  variables  was  checked  before 

proceeding with Structural Equation modelling (SEM). Basically, it is a framework of different 

multivariate  statistical  method  and  consist  of  different  domains  like  econometrics,  statistics 

psychology,  and  biology.  It  includes  correlation/regression,  simultaneous  equation,  factor 

analysis, path analysis and measurement theory. This model is used to test the relationships 

between  latent  and  observed  variables.  Latent  variables  are  the  variables  that  cannot  be 

observed  directly  or  unmeasured  variables  and  these  are  measured  by  connecting  to  the 

29 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
observed  variables.  Observed  variables  are  the  variables  that  can  be  measured  (Beran  & 

Violato, 2010).  

Furthermore,  SEM  is  the  combination  of  two  components:  measurement  model  and 

structural model. In measurement model, confirmatory factor analysis (CFA) is used based on 

SEM.  One  of  the  main  advantages  of  CFA  is  it  helps  in  bridging  the  often-observed  gap 

between theory and observation (R.O. Mueller., 2001). CFA deals with the latent variables, “it 

extracts  the  latent  constructs  from  other  variables  and  share  the  variance  with  related 

variables” (Fan et al., 2016). On the other hand, Path Analysis is very useful tool for testing 

the hypothesis with both casual effects direct and indirect. Mediation is one of the common 

functions  of  the  path  analysis,  where  variables  affect  directly  or  indirectly  to  the  outcome 

through other variables (Fan et al., n.d.). Like in this study, Resilience is playing a mediation 

role and we want to see indirect effect of personality traits on innovation competence through 

it.

The analysis of Creativity, Resilience and Personality Traits 

Table 7 presents the structural equation modelling analysis of resilience, personality traits and

unit would increase creativity by the predictors’ respective beta coefficient through resilience.

Discussion of Findings 

This study investigates the role of resilience on the relationship between the big five personality 

traits on the five dimensions of innovation competences. It was hypothesized that resilience 

mediates  the  impact  of  big  five  personality  traits  on  creativity,  critical  thinking,  initiative, 

networking,  and  teamwork.  In  the  following  paragraphs,  findings  of  study  results  are 

appropriately discussed. 

In this study, openness to experience was found to indirect predict creativity, critical 

thinking,  initiative teamwork and networking  thorough resilience. These results corroborate 

the findings of similar previous studies on personality traits and creativity, and creative abilities 

(Amin et al., 2020; Hirsh & Peterson, 2008; Kaufman et al., 2016; Raja & Johns, 2010) and 

critical thinking (Beaty et al., 2016; Feist, 2019; Yao & Li, 2021). One possible explanation 

could be that creativity which involves the generation of new methods, ideas and solutions may 

come with so many setbacks and failures before the final breakthrough. Thus, people high on 

openness are better able to cope with the stress associated with failures because of their rich 

experience with similar situations. This stress coping ability helps increases the chances of their 

creative  process.  Another  possible  explanation  could  be  that  opened  people  with  their  high 

level of imagination and intellect are better able to explore a variety of resources available to 

support  them  through  their  period  of  adversity.  Equipped  with  knowledge  of  our  people  in 

similar  difficult  situation  survived,  opened  people  become  encouraged  that  would  sure  sail 

through their dark days/hours and tough times don’t forever. So, we may imply that that the 

more opened to experience, the higher the level of resilience and thus the higher the level of 

innovative competences 

Another 

important  finding 

is 

that  resilience  also  mediates 

the 

impact  of 

conscientiousness on creativity, critical thinking, initiative teamwork and networking thorough 

resilience.  This  supports  evidence  from  previous  studies  on  personality  traits  and 

innovativeness and creativity (Ali, 2019; Amin et al., 2020; Chen, 2016)     and initiative (Saatci 

& Okvaci, 2020b). This can be explained in part that innovation and problem solving would 

require taking actions to implement new ideas and these actions can become very stressful due 

to uncertainties and risks. Focus, discipline and attention would therefore become helpful to 

manage such difficult and stressful endeavors. Highly conscientious people are very focused, 

disciplined  and  result  oriented.  Hence,  they  can  manage  these  difficulties  in  the  way  of 

innovation and then deliver the much-desired solutions to specific problems. So, we might say 

42 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
that the more conscientious an individual is, the higher his level of resilience and the higher 

the level of resilience. the more creative he becomes. 

In addition, the result of this study shows that the impact of extraversion on innovation 

competences is also mediated by resilience. These results are like those obtained in the studies 

by Amin et al. (2020), Furnham & Bachtiar (2008) and Saatci & Ovaci (2020).   Social network: 

colleagues,  collaborators,  and  friends,  can  provide  valuable  support  for  managing  adverse 

situations  and  recovering  from  adversities.  The  bigger  the  network,  the  more  support  is 

available  to  extraverted  individuals  during  their  trying  times.  With  larger  social  network, 

extraverted people can increase their resilience level. Another perspective to this explanation 

is that as part of the process of innovation, creative people must be mobilized to execute and 

operationalize new ideas to achieve the desired solutions, products, or services. Here, again 

extraversion  becomes  a  vantage  to  leverage  to  build  great  team  and  receive  feedback  from 

others on innovative initiatives. Large social cycle provides support for resilience, and in turn 

promotes teamwork and networking. 

On the question of whether resilience mediates the relationship between agreeableness 

and  innovation  competences,  this  study  found  that  resilience  mediates  the  role  between 

agreeableness and innovation competences.  This result goes in line with those of Ali (2019), 

Batey (2008) and Yao & Li (2021). Though the link between agreeableness and resilience may 

not be too clear, however one might explain that highly agreeable people can build congenial 

relationship in harmonious atmosphere and thus get acceptances from others. This acceptance 

provides  the  much-needed  psychological  relief  and  calmness  when  handling  social  conflict 

arising from collaborative activities.  

Expectedly, the mediating role of resilience on the impact of neuroticism on innovation 

competences is clear. The study confirms that the negative effect of neuroticism on innovation 

competences is mediated by resilience. We find similar result with (Ali, 2019). The observed 

relationship between resilience, neuroticism and innovation competences might be explained 

this  way:  being  resilient  requires  a  lot  of  emotional  stability.  Neuroticism  personalities  are 

overreactive with so much negative feelings. Thus, this emotional instability might make them 

less resilient and their therefore reduce their level of teamwork, networking, and creativity.

Conclusion 

The main goal of this study was to investigate the impact of resilience on the influence of Big 

five personality traits on all the five dimensions of innovation competences – creativity, critical 

thinking, networking, and teamwork. Using structural equation modelling, the results of this 

investigation show that resilience mediates the effect of big five personality traits on the five 

dimensions of innovation competences 

First,  openness  to  experiences  increases  the  students'  level  of  creativity,  critical 

thinking, networking, initiative, and networking through resilience. This implies that students 

with high tendency of exploring different interests and activities are more likely to cope with 

stress and overcome difficulties better. And this would support their level of resilience and then 

enhance their level of innovation competences. 

Second,  conscientiousness  indirectly  and  positively  predicts  creativity,  critical 

thinking, initiatives, networking, and teamwork. The more dutiful and focused the students are 

the higher their capacity to overcome adversity and then the higher their levels of competences. 

Thirdly,  people’s  level  of  agreeableness  (trustworthiness)  sustains  heir  cordial 

relationships. This warm relationship would provide them with support them to pull through 

adversity. And this increase in level of resilience would increase their resilience level during 

adversity positively impacts their impacts their level of resilience which in turn supports their 

innovation competences. The fourth finding relates to extraversion the social trait. Likewise, 

the  more  of  it  a  student  possess,  the  more  resilient  and  the  higher  his  level  innovation 

competences. 

Conversely,  neuroticism  which  negatively  and  indirectly  influences  innovation 

competences. Students with high level of emotional instability are less resilient and eventually 

displays low level of innovation competences.

Implications 

There could be several implications of this study related to organization, universities, and 

government of the economy. 

1.  Theoretical implication of this study is that it contributes in the existing theory by 

determining the role of resilience as a mediator in the innovative competence and personality 

traits.   

2.  The second implication of this study can be that it can help the universities to design the 

various learning strategies and pedagogy methods like problem solving activities in the 

context of discipline-based curriculum and courses that can develop and enhance the 

innovative competences and resilience among students.  

3.  The third implication is for business or employers that they should design training programs 

for  employees  that  can  foster  the  innovative  behaviors  and  enhance  the  resilience  in  the 

workplace. So, they would be better able to cope with stress and adversity.  

4.  The fourth implication of this study could be for the government to design the policies that 

could encourage the institutions and organizations to design the programs that can enhance the 

innovative behavior in current and future labor force so that they can productively contribute 

towards economy. 

5.  This study can help the adolescents, universities, and employers to understand the importance 

of resilience which can help the individuals to prevent from mental issues like depression and 

enhance the productivity in long-term.  

6.  Finally, this study can be helpful for the counselors and psychologist that provides individual 

or  group  counseling  services  to  help  them  to  relieve  the  stress  improve  well-being  among 

individuals.  It will help them by identifying, understand, express, and manage their emotions 

through various activities and short-trainings and support.  

45 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Limitations of the study 

Nothing is perfect in the world; everything has positive as well as negative side. Same like this 

study has few limitations but those limitations are not potential and not affecting the results. 

These can be overcome in the future research.  

1.  First  limitation  of  this  study  is  that  due  to  the  practical  constraints  e.g.,  survey  length  and 

respondent's  burden,  single  item  measure  is  used  for  personality  traits.  Even  single  item 

measure is suggested based on certain conditions, the fact that multi-item measure is better than 

using single item measure. 

2.  Second limitation is, all the data was collected on self-reported measures that means individuals 

rated themselves based on self-understandings, which can cause response bias and increase the 

chances  of  low  validity  and  reliability  of  the  scale.  However,  in  this  study,  reliability  and 

validity was more than standard acceptable value. But for the in-depth insights, responses of 

the other people on the reaction or the activities of individuals will be considered better such 

as family members, teachers, and employers etc. 

3.  Third limitation is that the lack of control variables, linked to participants' demographics, which 

might have important explanatory effects that are not addressed in this study. They were only 

used in descriptive studies. 

Future Research 

Following recommendations are provided for the future work: 

1.  Longitudinal research can also be work for this type of study. 

2.  Comparatives approaches can be done based on demographic variables, such as age, gender or 

work experience. 

3.  Cultural factors can also be considered as one of the main variables in future study because 

personality traits and resilience can differ based on culture. Stress and depression vary with 

nation  to  nation.  It  can  be  because  of  collectivistic  and  individualistic  society  based  on  the 

Hofstede model.  

4.  It is recommended to use multi-items measure for the any variables that are mentioned in this 

study. 

5.  Based on the limitation, it is also recommended to collect data that is not self-reported.
